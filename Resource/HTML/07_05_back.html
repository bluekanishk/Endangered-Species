Glossary Terms: J to L

K

 

kelp    A type of brown seaweed. Kelp forests are large areas of thick seaweed under the water.

keystone species    A type of animal or plant that is vital to the well-being, growth, and distribution of other species within its area.

krill    Small crustaceans that look like tiny shrimps that live in large groups, called swarms, in the Arctic and Antarctic waters. Krill are the main diet of many animals in these oceans, such as some whales.

L

 

larvae    (plural of larva) A stage of growth in the life cycle of most invertebrates, amphibians, and fish. Larvae look completely different from the adult animals, for example caterpillars (young butterflies), or tadpoles (young frogs).

lead shot    The type of bullet used in some guns, especially those used for shooting birds and animals. The bullet is made of lead which is poisonous.

lek    A gathering place for males of the same species, where they call and display to attract the attention of the females. For example, male kakapos gather in a lek when they are trying to attract a female.

lichen    An organism formed from a fungus and algae that grows on trees and rocks.

line fishing    see long-line fishing

livestock    Animals, such as cows and sheep, that are kept and reared for profit by farmers.

logging    The cutting down and clearing of forest to sell the wood.

long-line fishing    This is a type of fishing where many short lines with hooks to catch fish are attached to a much longer line that can stretch up to 100 miles (150 kilometers). The long line can have thousands of short lines with hooks. Long-line fishing can accidentally catch and kill turtles, seabirds, and sharks.
