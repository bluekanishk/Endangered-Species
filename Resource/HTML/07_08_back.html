Glossary Terms: S to U

S

 

salamander    A tailed amphibian.

savanna    Flat grassland usually in warmer climates.

scavengers    Animals that eat carrion, the remains of animals killed by other animals.

sedimentation    When tiny bits of solid material, such as rocks and shells, fall to the bottom of an area of water such as a river or lake.

semi-arid    A place that is usually dry and has scrubby vegetation, but which does have a small amount of rain each year.

serrated    Having a jagged edge, like a saw. Serrated usually describes teeth that are used for tearing and eating meat.

settlers    People who travel to an area and build on it so they can live there.

sewage    Liquid and solid waste produced by humans.

shaman    A holy or spiritual leader who uses magic to heal the sick and communicate with the dead.

silt    A type of sediment consisting of fine particles, smaller than sand.

solar-powered    Equipment powered by electricity that is made using energy from the sun.

soy plantations    A large area of land where soy bean crops are grown.

spawning    Producing eggs.

sperm    (or spermatozoa) The cell produced by male animals, which fertilizes an egg cell of a female to produce a new life.

splayed    When an animal's hooves can spread out to make it easier for that animal to walk on muddy, wet, or swampy ground.

sponges    Simple marine animals that live on the seabed and coral reefs. Their body has many holes through which water can pass. They filter food from the water.

standing water    Water found in ponds and lakes that does not move or have tides.

sub-tropical    Relating to the areas north and south of the tropics, where there is a seasonal climate.

sub-Saharan    The countries of Africa, south of the Sahara.

sub-Antarctic    The oceans and islands just north of the Antarctic.

sub-Arctic    The oceans and islands just south of the Arctic.

suckle    When a baby drinks milk from its mother.

sustainably    When something can be harvested while at the same time the land or habitat can be protected for the future and the plants and animals unharmed.

sustainable farming    When land is farmed in such a way that it remains productive and healthy and is protected from bad practices, such as the use of too many pesticides and fertilizers, and where the wildlife is unharmed.

sustainable tourism    Tourism that does not harm an area by pollution, or by taking away space from the wildlife that lives there, or by destroying the habitat with hotels and other developments.

T

 

temperate    A place where the temperature is never scorching hot or freezing cold for any length of time and there are seasons such as summer and winter.

tick-borne    A disease or illness that is passed on to an animal or human by ticks, small animals related to spiders.

tundra    Vast cold, frozen areas found near the Arctic with low growing vegetation and a short growing season.

traditional medicines    Medicines that have been used by people without any medical training for many hundreds of years. They are not based on science and are often made from herbs and plants. In some countries traditional medicines are made from the body parts of endangered animals and many are being made and sold illegally.

tropical    Places near the Equator that have warm or hot temperatures all year round.

U

 

ultrasound    High frequency or high-pitched sounds that humans cannot hear.

urban    City living; an area where there are lots of homes, offices, shops, and traffic, and where huge numbers of people live.
