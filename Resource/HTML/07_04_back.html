Glossary Terms: G to I

G

 

grub    The larva or young of an insect, such as an ant.

H

 

herbivore    An animal that eats only plant foods, such as fruit, grass, and leaves.

hibernate    When an animal remains in one place for the winter. The animal finds somewhere warm and safe, such as a cave or burrow, then falls into a deep sleep. During this time the animal survives by feeding on its body fat. Some animals wake from hibernation and move around, and then go back into hibernation again.

hierarchy    In a group of animals, the ranking order from the lowest to the top.

Hindu    A person who believes in Hinduism, which is a religion and a way of life.

hybrid    The offspring of two different animal parents or plant varieties. For example, a beefalo is a hybrid and it is the offspring of a buffalo and a cow.

hydro-electric    Electricity that is created using water power.

I

 

incubate    To keep eggs at a certain temperature so that the young inside will develop and hatch.

Inuit    A member of the people that originated and still live in the northern most part of North America.

Inuvialuit    Inuits who live in the western Canadian Arctic region.

invertebrates    An animal that does not have a backbone, such as a beetle, worm, or octopus.

irrigate    To keep land in dry areas well watered by means of pipes, sprinklers, or other water systems.
