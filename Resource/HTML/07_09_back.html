Glossary Terms: V to Z

V

 

vegetarian    People that eat mainly plant foods and do not eat meat or fish.

venom    A poison that can kill or injure, and that is used by some animals, such as snakes, to protect themselves or to kill prey.

vertebrates    Animals that have a backbone such as fish, mammals, reptiles, and birds.

W

 

wet forest    Forest that grows in areas of high rainfall, and where the roots of trees may be covered in water for part of the year.

wetlands    Areas that always have very wet, boggy, or swampy ground.

wildlife corridors    Safe routes for animals allowing them to pass from one fragment of habitat to another, the routes having been replanted with trees or grass.

WWF    World Wildlife Fund (for Nature) A charitable organization set up in 1961 to support environmental and conservation projects, to protect animals and habitats. Today it has over 5 million supporters worldwide.

Z

 

zoologists    Scientists who study zoology, the study of animal life.

zooxanthellae    Tiny algae that live in coral and provide it with food.
