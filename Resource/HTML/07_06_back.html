Glossary Terms: M to O

M

 

mangroves    Place where a type of evergreen tree called a mangrove grows. They grow in shallow, wet areas beside the sea.

Maoris    The native people of New Zealand.

marinas    Harbors or docks where working and leisure boats moor.

marsupials    Mammals whose new-born live in the female's pouch, where they are protected and can feed on their mother's milk until they are old enough to move around by themselves.

migration    Regular journeys, often over long distances, taken by some animals, such as whales, birds, and butterflies. The migrations are usually to reach warmer regions where there is food or places where females can mate and give birth.

molluscs    Animals with no backbone and a soft body that has some sort of shell to protect it, such as snails, slugs, mussels, squid, or octopus.

monotremes    Mammals that lay eggs rather than give birth to live young, and include the duck-billed platypus and echidna.

muzzle    The part of an animal's head that juts out, including the mouth and nose, such as that of the dog or wolf.

N

 

naturalist    A person who studies animals and plants.

nestling    A young animal that still lives in the nest being cared for by a parent.

nocturnal    Animals that are active at night and that rest or sleep during the day.

O

 

old growth forest    Ancient forests that are hundreds of years old and have large trees and a rich variety of plants and animals living in them.

omnivorous    Animals that eat a mixed diet of meats and plants.

overfishing    When local or commercial fishermen do not leave enough fish in the water to breed, so that the amount of fish being caught is not being replaced by new fish.

over grazing    To allow animals to graze so much in an area that the grass or vegetation does not have a chance to regrow.

overwintering    Animals that remain in the same habitat during the winter.

ovoviviparous    Female animals that keep their eggs inside their body, where the eggs hatch and the mother gives birth to live young. Some fish, amphibians, and reptiles reproduce in this way.
