Glossary Terms: A to C

A

 

acid rain    Rain that contains harmful chemicals that have blown into the atmosphere from power stations, factories, and our homes, that have been burning coal, oil and other fuels. Acid rain can damage the leaves and roots of plants, and harm aquatic animals in rivers and lakes.

algae    The simplest forms of plant life, found in water or damp places, usually green in color.

Antarctic    The continent at the South Pole that is covered in snow and ice all year round.

anthrax    An infectious, deadly disease caused by bacteria being passed from one animal or human to another.

arboreal    Animals that spend most or all of their time living in trees and not coming down to the ground.

Arctic    The most northerly part of the planet, covered in ice all year and has freezing temperatures.

arid    Dry and parched.

B

 

baleen plates    In some whales, the comb-like plates hanging from the upper jaw that are used to sieve food from sea water. These plates are often referred to as whalebone.

biologists    Scientists who study humans and animals.

bleaching    When corals lose their zooxanthellae and they turn white and die. This is caused by an increase in the temperature of the water.

blubber    A thick layer of fat under the skin that many animals have, such as penguins and walruses, that live in icy habitats. The fat helps to keep the animal warm in freezing temperatures.

bony fish    Fish that have a skeleton made of bones rather than cartilage. Most fish are bony fish. Manta rays and sharks have a cartilage rather than a bony skeleton.

burrows    Tunnels made under the ground by some animals where they live and store food.

bushmeat    The meat of wild animals, such as gorillas. Usually the animals are killed illegally.

C

 

camouflaged    When an animal is difficult to see because it blends in with its surroundings due to the color and pattern of its coat.

canopy    The branches of trees that form the top layer, or roof, of a forest.

carapace    The shell or hard covering of an animal, such as a turtle.

carcass    The body of a dead animal.

carnivores    Animals that eat mainly meat. Most carnivores also eat some plant food.

carrion    The rotting flesh and body of dead animals.

cartilage    Tough, but flexible tissue found in the body. Cartilaginous fish, such as sharks, have a skeleton made of cartilage.

casque    A soft, spongy crest found on the head of birds, such as the cassowary.

cerrado    A large area of tropical grassland in Brazil that contains a wide range of plants, mammals, and birds.

CITES    The letters stand for Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora. CITES has an international treaty with 172 member countries.

CITES Appendix I    This is a list of species that cannot be traded commercially.

CITES Appendix II    This is a list of products from species that can be traded commercially, but only if it does not harm their survival.

civil war    A war between groups of people within the same country.

courtship    Special behavior in animals that are trying to attract a mate.

corridors    A link between two fragments of habitat that provides a safe route for animals to pass from one area to the other.

crustacean    An animal that lives in water that has a hard external skeleton, such as a crab or lobster
